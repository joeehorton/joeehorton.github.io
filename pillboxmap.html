Mapping the remaining pillboxes

Role —
Developer, Designer,
Photographer

After the evacuation of Dunkirk in 1940, Churchill ordered 30,000 pillboxes to be built across the British Isles in preparation for Hitler’s planned invasion of Britain. Today, only 9000 or so of these pillboxes remain.

Between 2004 and 2008 The Archaeological Society of England conducted a field survey to locate and record these remaining pillboxes. You can access their database as a .kmz file that is free to download on The Pillbox Study Group. You could then upload that file to Google Earth. This is currently the only way to see the remaining pillboxes on a map. This experience is slow, tedious, and impractical.

I am working on a solution to this problem. Using Mapbox I am designing and developing an interactive online map, which would enable one to determine the location, type, description, history and accessibility of each and every remaining pillbox in the UK.

This is an enormous project. With a grant application to the Arts Council of England underway and a potential partnership with The Imperial War Museum, my intention is for The Pillbox Map to be completed by the end of 2020.
